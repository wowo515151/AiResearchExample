Graphcore: Scaling Machine Intelligence Through Specialized IPU Technology

Graphcore has established itself as a significant force in the semiconductor industry, specifically focusing on the specialized hardware required to power the next generation of artificial intelligence. Founded in 2016 in Bristol, United Kingdom, by Nigel Toon and Simon Knowles, the company was built on the premise of creating processors designed from the ground up for machine intelligence. Following a period of rapid growth and substantial capital raises, Graphcore was acquired by SoftBank Group Corp on July 11, 2024, and continues to operate under its original name as a wholly owned subsidiary.

The core of Graphcore’s value proposition lies in its Intelligence Processing Unit (IPU). Unlike traditional processors, the IPU architecture is specifically designed to run current machine learning models significantly faster across various servers, software, and processors. This specialized technology enables businesses to handle emerging and complex machine intelligence workloads more efficiently. The company’s mission is centered on a future of "democratized intelligence," aiming to provide the computational power necessary to enhance human potential and foster a more informed and sustainable world.

Graphcore’s trajectory is marked by a series of highly successful funding rounds that saw its valuation rise from its early stages to a multi-billion dollar scale. The company has raised a total of $712 million through various stages of investment. Key milestones include a $200 million Series D round that established Graphcore as a "unicorn" with a valuation of $1.7 billion, and a subsequent Series E round of $222 million led by the Ontario Teachers’ Pensions Plan Board, which brought its valuation to $2.77 billion. These rounds attracted a prestigious roster of investors, including Sequoia Capital, Atomico, Bosch, Microsoft, BMW, and various AI luminaries such as Demis Hassabis and Ilya Sutskever.

On the product front, Graphcore has demonstrated consistent innovation and market traction. The company successfully transitioned from early IPU products and PCIe cards to the second-generation IPU-Machine M2000 platform, which features MK2 GC200 IPU processors and offers an eightfold increase in performance. Graphcore’s technology is already integrated into major ecosystems; its IPUs are available through Microsoft Azure and can be purchased via Dell.

As a global player in the general-purpose semiconductor industry, Graphcore maintains a significant operational footprint with offices in Bristol, London, Cambridge, Hsinchu, and Gdansk. With approximately 500 employees, the company remains a critical architect in the AI compute landscape, providing the specialized infrastructure necessary to drive breakthroughs in machine intelligence.
