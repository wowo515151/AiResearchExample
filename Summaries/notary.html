Addressing the Complexity of Software Supply Chains

As modern enterprises navigate increasingly complex distributed software environments—spanning pipelines, cloud services, and edge devices—traditional security controls are often insufficient. These legacy approaches frequently generate noisy signals and leave significant gaps as artifacts traverse various environments. Codenotary addresses these vulnerabilities by moving away from episodic, manual assurance toward continuous, automated trust mechanisms.

To solve these challenges, Codenotary employs a hybrid technological approach that combines deterministic methods with artificial intelligence. By pairing cryptographic provenance and immutable attestations with AI triage, the platform can automate policy decisions and surface high-confidence anomalies in real time. This integration significantly reduces "noisy" alerts, allowing security teams to focus their resources on genuine risks rather than false positives.

Technical Framework and Operational Impact

The company’s technical approach is built upon the integration of Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), attestations, and policy-as-code. This framework provides three primary advantages for enterprise security:

  • Rapid Validation: The ability to validate software components during the build and deployment phases.
  • Continuous Enforcement: The capacity to enforce security policies as artifacts move across different environments.
  • Scalable Telemetry: The generation of logs and proofs that support comprehensive audit and compliance requirements.

By embedding continuous verification directly into CI/CD pipelines and runtime environments, organizations can achieve measurable reductions in both the mean time to detect and the mean time to remediate security threats.

As software supply chain security becomes a fundamental piece of cross-functional infrastructure, Codenotary is well-positioned to lead the transition toward automated, real-time enterprise trust.
