Weights & Biases: From Leading MLOps Platform to Strategic Acquisition by CoreWeave

Weights & Biases (W&B) has established itself as a cornerstone of the machine learning ecosystem, evolving from a specialized tool for model training into a comprehensive, developer-first MLOps platform. Following a period of rapid growth and significant capital infusion, the company has reached a major milestone in its corporate trajectory: the completion of its acquisition by CoreWeave, Inc. This acquisition, finalized in May 2025, marks a significant consolidation in the AI infrastructure sector, merging a premier software layer with a high-performance AI cloud platform.

Prior to its acquisition, Weights & Biases demonstrated exceptional market momentum and scalability. The company reported a massive expansion of its user base, growing from 100,000 users following its 2021 Series C round to over 700,000 machine learning practitioners. This growth was supported by a significant strategic investment of $50 million, which valued the company at $1.25 billion. This funding round, led by prominent investors Daniel Gross and Nat Friedman, included participation from several major venture firms such as Sapphire Ventures, Coatue, and Insight Partners.

The company’s value proposition lies in its ability to provide an end-to-end workflow for machine learning development. As the industry shifted its focus toward large language models (LLMs), Weights & Biases successfully pivoted to meet the needs of generative AI. The launch of "W&B Prompts" was a key component of this evolution, providing developers with the necessary tools to fine-tune, prompt engineer, and debug foundational models. By enabling practitioners to better understand every step of an LLM program, the platform has become essential for accelerating the time it takes to move models from research to production.

The adoption of Weights & Biases is widespread among the industry's most influential players. The platform has been utilized by nearly all major generative AI model builders, including Meta, OpenAI, and HuggingFace. Notably, OpenAI was the company’s first customer, and the platform has been used to train many of the world's most ambitious models. In total, the Weights & Biases MLOps platform has tracked nearly 300 million hours of machine learning experiments for a diverse client list that includes NVIDIA, Toyota Research Institute, Volkswagen, and Square.

The acquisition by CoreWeave is designed to create a powerful synergy between hardware and software. By combining CoreWeave's accelerated computing cloud with Weights & Biases' developer-centric software, the unified entity aims to provide a full-stack solution for the "inference era." While CoreWeave integrates the platform, W&B remains committed to interoperability, ensuring customers maintain the flexibility to choose their preferred deployment options, infrastructure providers, and foundational models.

For investors, the trajectory of Weights & Biases represents a significant success story in the AI infrastructure space. The company transitioned from a vital tool for academic researchers into a standard-setting platform for enterprise-grade machine learning, ultimately providing a high-value exit through its integration into CoreWeave’s expanding AI cloud ecosystem.
