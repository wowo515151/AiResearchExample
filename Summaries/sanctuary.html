Investment Profile: Sanctuary AI

Sanctuary AI is a Vancouver-based developer specializing in the creation of robotic systems and software designed for autonomous labor. Founded in 2018, the company operates within the business and productivity software industry, focusing on the development of general-purpose robots. Sanctuary AI’s core mission is to address critical modern workforce challenges, including labor shortages, high employee turnover, and workplace safety concerns. By implementing autonomous robotic labor, the company aims to increase overall productivity and improve the employee experience by fostering a safer work environment and reducing workplace-related injuries.

The company’s technological framework is built upon several advanced developments, including dynamic system controllers with online machine learning. To facilitate efficient robot training, Sanctuary AI utilizes synthetic robot data generation and model training. Furthermore, the company has developed specialized systems and methods for time-of-flight touch sensors, which are integrated into their robotic systems.

From a financial perspective, Sanctuary AI is a private company that has successfully raised $119 million to date. The company has attracted a significant group of 36 investors, including notable names such as Zeon Ventures, Accenture Ventures, ALM Ventures, BDC Capital, and InBC Investment Corp. Their most recent funding milestone was a Series A - VI round, which raised $1.55 million on April 17, 2026. In terms of strategic growth, the company completed a Corporate Asset Purchase of the intellectual property portfolio of Giant.AI on December 20, 2023.

As Sanctuary AI scales, it operates within a highly competitive landscape of robotics and artificial intelligence firms. Its primary competitors in the humanoid and autonomous robotics sector include Agility Robotics, Figure, Apptronik, 1X, and UBTECH.

Currently, Sanctuary AI maintains a workforce of 164 employees. As the demand for autonomous labor solutions continues to evolve, the company remains positioned at the intersection of advanced AI and physical robotics, aiming to transform how industries approach manual labor and workplace safety.
