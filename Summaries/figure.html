Figure Secures $1 Billion in Series C Funding, Achieving a $39 Billion Valuation

Figure, a San Jose-based developer of general-purpose humanoid robots, has announced a major milestone in its mission to revolutionize the labor market. The company has exceeded $1 billion in committed capital through its Series C financing round, bringing its post-money valuation to $39 billion. This significant capital infusion is intended to accelerate the deployment of humanoid robots into real-world environments at scale, addressing critical labor shortages and reducing the necessity for human workers in unsafe job roles.

The Series C round was led by Parkway Venture Capital. The group of investors includes several prominent industry leaders and strategic partners, such as NVIDIA, Intel Capital, Qualcomm Ventures, Salesforce, T-Mobile Ventures, and LG Technology Ventures. Other notable participants include Brookfield Asset Management, Macquarie Capital, Align Ventures, and Tamarack Global. This diverse backing underscores Figure’s position as a market leader in the burgeoning field of embodied intelligence.

To achieve its growth objectives, Figure is focusing its capital on three strategic pillars designed to unlock the full potential of its AI platform, Helix. First, the company is scaling the production of its humanoid robots through its BotQ manufacturing facility, aiming to deploy units into both residential homes and commercial operations to assist with various manual tasks. Second, Figure is investing in next-generation GPU infrastructure to accelerate the training and simulation of its core models, specifically focusing on perception, reasoning, and control. Third, the company is launching advanced data collection initiatives, utilizing human video and multimodal sensory inputs to help Helix better understand and navigate complex, dynamic environments.

Founded in 2022, Figure has rapidly scaled to a workforce of approximately 500 employees. To date, the company has raised a total of $2.34 billion. Its technology is positioned to serve a wide range of industries, including manufacturing, logistics, warehousing, and retail.

As the company moves toward commercializing autonomous humanoid robots, it faces a competitive landscape that includes players such as Agility Robotics, Universal Robots, and Onward Robotics. However, with its recent funding and the advancement of its Helix AI system, Figure is well-positioned to lead the transition toward a future where robotic assistance becomes a seamless part of daily life and industrial operations.
