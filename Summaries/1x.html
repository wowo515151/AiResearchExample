1X Technologies: Scaling the Future of Embodied AI and Humanoid Robotics

As the robotics sector experiences an unprecedented surge driven by advances in artificial intelligence, 1X Technologies is positioning itself at the forefront of the movement. The Norwegian-based robotics startup, which has evolved from its origins as Halodi Robotics, is currently preparing for a massive capital raise that could redefine its market standing. The company is reportedly seeking up to $1 billion at a valuation of at least $10 billion—a figure that represents more than twelve times its valuation from just one year ago.

From Industrial Roots to the Consumer Home

1X Technologies has undergone a significant strategic evolution since its founding in 2014. While its early work focused on enterprise-centered androids—such as EVE, a wheeled robot designed for industrial tasks—the company’s current mission is increasingly centered on the consumer market. This shift is embodied in their flagship product, NEO, a bipedal humanoid designed to assist with domestic chores. NEO is engineered to perform a variety of household tasks, including making coffee, folding laundry, and vacuuming, with a primary focus on safety and ease of interaction in a home environment.

The company’s product roadmap continues to advance with the development of "Neo Gamma." As the successor to the Neo Beta prototype, Gamma features a softer, more approachable design utilizing knitted nylon suits to reduce the risk of injury during human interaction. This focus on safety is central to 1X's mission of deploying "Smart Behavior" androids that can live and work alongside people, particularly as a tool for supporting older adults in maintaining independent living.

The Competitive Edge in Embodied AI

What distinguishes 1X from other major players in the humanoid space, such as Tesla’s Optimus or Figure AI, is its specific emphasis on the consumer sector and its approach to "Embodied AI." Rather than relying on rigid coding, 1X focuses on robots that learn and adapt through real-world experience. This approach to data collection and embodied intelligence is a primary driver for the company’s strategic direction and has attracted significant interest from industry leaders.

The company's manufacturing capabilities are also scaling rapidly. The NEO Factory in Hayward, California, is established as America’s first true vertically integrated high-volume humanoid robot factory, providing the infrastructure necessary to transition from prototyping to commercial-scale production.

Financial Momentum and Strategic Partnerships

The financial trajectory of 1X Technologies has been remarkably rapid. In March 2023, the company completed a Series A extension led by the OpenAI Startup Fund, with participation from Tiger Global. This was followed by a $100 million Series B round led by EQT Ventures, which included participation from Samsung NEXT, the Nistad Group, and Skagerak Capital. In total, 1X has raised over $125 million in less than 12 months.

The involvement of OpenAI underscores the synergy between 1X’s hardware goals and the broader evolution of AI. For investors, the current pursuit of a $1 billion funding round represents a high-stakes attempt to capture a leading share of a burgeoning market. If successful, 1X will join an elite group of well-funded humanoid developers aiming to solve global labor shortages in sectors ranging from logistics to elder care.

Investor Outlook

While the potential for transformation is vast, 1X faces the significant industrial challenge of making humanoid robots commercially viable. Achieving large-scale success will require the company to prove its technology at scale, specifically regarding reliability, cost-efficiency, and safety. However, with a robust manufacturing foundation in California, a clear product evolution from industrial to domestic use, and significant backing from global tech leaders, 1X Technologies remains a pivotal player in the race to bring humanoid robotics into the everyday lives of consumers.
