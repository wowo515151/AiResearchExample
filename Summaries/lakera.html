Lakera: A Strategic Leader in Generative AI Security and Market Consolidation

As enterprises increasingly integrate artificial intelligence into their core operations, the necessity for robust security frameworks has become a primary concern for stakeholders. Lakera, a San Francisco-based company in the Network Management Software industry, has emerged as a specialized leader in this critical domain. Founded in 2021, Lakera has developed a security platform specifically engineered to protect Generative AI systems, including large language models (LLMs), agents, and multimodal applications.

Lakera’s technology addresses the unique vulnerabilities inherent in generative models. The company’s software provides essential runtime protection, red-teaming, and threat data, enabling clients to defend against sophisticated cyber threats such as prompt injections, data leakage, and jailbreaks. By providing these safeguards, Lakera allows organizations to deploy AI systems securely while maintaining compliance with emerging regulatory standards.

The company’s growth has been fueled by significant capital interest from prominent investors. To date, Lakera has raised a total of $33.9 million through six funding rounds. Its investor group includes notable names such as Atomico, Citi Ventures, Creative Destruction Lab, Dropbox Ventures, and Fly Ventures. Notably, Atomico has played a key role as a lead investor in the company's Series A funding.

The strategic value of Lakera’s specialized security offerings was underscored in late 2025 when the company was acquired by Check Point Software Technologies. This acquisition marks a significant milestone in the consolidation of the AI security market, as major cybersecurity players seek to integrate advanced protections for generative AI into their existing ecosystems.

With a workforce of 70 employees, Lakera has successfully navigated a competitive landscape that includes players such as Portal26, WhyLabs, CalypsoAI, ImmuniWeb, and Saporo. Through its focus on protecting the AI lifecycle and ensuring the integrity of automated agents, Lakera has established itself as a vital component in the evolving cybersecurity infrastructure required for the age of artificial intelligence.
