Harvey: The Emergence of the Legal Operating System through Agentic AI

As the legal industry undergoes a fundamental shift toward automation, Harvey is positioning itself at the forefront of this transformation. Moving beyond simple AI assistance, Harvey is establishing itself as the "operating system" for legal and professional services. By leveraging purpose-built AI agents capable of executing complex, end-to-end workflows, the company is redefining how law firms and in-house legal teams manage high-volume, intricate tasks.

The Shift from Assistance to Execution

The core differentiator of Harvey’s technology lies in its "Agentic" approach. While traditional AI tools often serve as mere assistants to human practitioners, Harvey’s purpose-built agents are designed to execute workflows from start to finish. These agents operate across various critical domains, including M&A, due diligence, contract drafting, document review, compliance, and litigation.

According to Winston Weinberg, CEO and co-founder of Harvey, AI is transitioning from a tool that assists lawyers to the very system through which legal work is performed. This allows legal professionals to shift their focus away from routine manual paperwork and toward high-level judgment, strategy, and complex reasoning. Currently, more than 25,000 custom agents are operational on the platform, with customers increasingly deploying "long-horizon" agents to handle multi-step workflows such as fund formation.

Proven Market Traction and Global Scale

Harvey has demonstrated significant market penetration and rapid scalability. The company currently serves over 1,300 customers across more than 60 countries. This client base includes a majority of the AmLaw 100, over 500 in-house legal teams, and 50 asset management firms. Notable recent customer wins and expansions include NBCUniversal, HSBC, Corrs Chambers Westgarth, DLA Piper International, and McCann Fitzgerald.

The platform’s utility is underscored by its massive user base, with more than 100,000 lawyers worldwide running their most critical work through Harvey. To facilitate collaboration, the platform offers "Shared Spaces," allowing legal teams to securely coordinate work with external partners, and an "Agent Builder" tool currently in early access to empower users to create bespoke solutions.

Robust Financial Position and Tier-One Backing

From an investment perspective, Harvey’s trajectory is marked by significant capital infusion and high-conviction backing from the world’s most prominent venture capital firms. The company recently announced a $200 million funding round co-led by returning investors GIC and Sequoia. This round values Harvey at $11 billion and includes participation from a prestigious roster of investors, including Andreessen Horowitz, Coatue, Kleiner Perkins, and the OpenAI Startup Fund.

With this recent round, Harvey has raised more than $1 billion in total funding. These capital reserves are being deployed to expand the suite of agents available to customers and to grow the company’s global embedded legal engineering teams.

Company Profile and Strategic Outlook

Founded in 2022 and headquartered in San Francisco, California, Harvey has grown to a workforce of approximately 960 employees. The company continues to expand its ecosystem through strategic moves, such as its recent merger and acquisition with Lume in March 2026.

As legal workflows move toward a model of autonomous execution, Harvey’s integration of domain-specific AI and professional-grade legal infrastructure places it in a unique position to lead the next decade of legal technology. For investors, Harvey represents a high-growth play in the enterprise AI sector, specifically targeting the massive and historically manual legal services market.
