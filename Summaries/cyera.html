Cyera Secures $600 Million Funding at $12 Billion Valuation to Anchor the "Trust Layer" of the AI Era

As enterprises worldwide accelerate their transition toward generative AI, a critical security gap has emerged between AI ambition and trusted implementation. Cyera, a leading data security company, has announced a $600 million funding round that quadruples the company’s valuation over the last 18 months, bringing its total valuation to $12 billion. This significant capital injection positions Cyera as one of the most valuable privately held security companies in the world, providing the "fuel" necessary to build the essential infrastructure required for the agentic AI era.

Addressing the AI Infrastructure Gap

The rapid adoption of AI has outpaced the development of the governance layers required to manage it. Currently, a structural gap exists in the industry: while significant investment has been poured into chips, models, and compute, there is a lack of infrastructure to govern what AI agents can see, learn from, and do. This lack of visibility creates significant risk; projections suggest that by 2026, 68% of organizations will be unable to distinguish between human activity and AI agent activity within their own systems.

Cyera is positioning itself as the "trust layer" that bridges this gap. By providing visibility and context, Cyera enables organizations to secure their data at rest, in motion, and in use, ensuring that AI adoption does not come at the cost of security or compliance.

A Unified and AI-Native Platform

Cyera’s technical defensibility lies in its AI-native platform, which is purpose-built to manage the complexities of modern data environments. The platform offers a unique convergence of Data Security Posture Management (DSPM), Data Loss Prevention (DLP), identity, and behavior. Key capabilities include:

  • High-Precision Discovery: The platform can discover and classify exabytes of data with over 95% precision, identifying sensitive records at risk without disrupting existing workflows.
  • Comprehensive Governance: Cyera provides a single platform to govern what AI can see and do, addressing the specific risks posed by autonomous AI agents.
  • Rapid Innovation: Within the last year alone, the company has shipped more than 100 new product capabilities to strengthen its comprehensive trust layer.

Exceptional Growth and Market Traction

Cyera’s financial and operational metrics reflect the urgent market demand for its solutions. The company has reported that its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) has tripled for three consecutive years. This growth is further evidenced by a 353% increase in its Fortune 500 customer base over the last 18 months.

The company’s physical and technological footprint has expanded aggressively. Cyera now employs approximately 1,500 people across 18 countries. To deepen its ability to govern AI at scale, the company has completed five strategic acquisitions in the last 18 months, most recently acquiring Ryft and Genie.

Strategic Investment and Leadership

The latest funding round was led by Evolution Equity Partners, with significant participation from Temasek, Cyberstarts, and a cohort of existing investors including Accel, AT&T Ventures, Blackstone, Coatue, and Spark Capital. In total, Cyera has raised over $2 billion to date.

The company was founded in 2021 by CEO Yotam Segev and CTO Tamar Bar-Ilan. Both founders are alumni of Israel’s elite Talpiot program and previously served in the military’s cyber intelligence Unit 8200. Under their leadership, Cyera has evolved from a startup into a critical infrastructure provider for global enterprises such as Paramount, Chipotle, and Valvoline.

For investors, Cyera represents a high-growth play at the intersection of cybersecurity and the AI revolution, tackling the fundamental challenge of maintaining data integrity and control in an increasingly automated world.
