Apptronik Secures $520 Million Series A-X Extension to Scale Humanoid Robotics Production

Austin, Texas — Apptronik, a leader in the development of AI-powered humanoid robotics, has announced a significant $520 million Series A-X funding round. This extension follows a highly successful, oversubscribed $415 million Series A raise in 2025, bringing the company’s total Series A funding to more than $935 million. With this latest infusion of capital, Apptronik has now raised nearly $1 billion to accelerate the production and deployment of its flagship humanoid robot, Apollo™.

Robust Investor Momentum and Valuation Growth

The Series A-X round underscores a period of rapid scaling and intense market interest. Notably, the extension was opened at a three-times multiple of the previous Series A valuation, a testament to the significant confidence expressed by both existing and new institutional investors.

The funding round saw continued participation from established backers, including B Capital, Google, Mercedes-Benz, and PEAK6. The company also welcomed high-profile new investors, including AT&T Ventures, John Deere, and the Qatar Investment Authority (QIA). This diverse cap table, comprising both strategic industry leaders and major sovereign wealth funds, highlights the broad market demand for embodied AI solutions.

Scaling Apollo™: From Pilot to Global Deployment

The primary objective for this new capital is the rapid ramp-up of Apollo™, the company’s award-winning humanoid robot. Apptronik intends to expand its global network of commercial and pilot deployments, specifically targeting sectors facing labor shortages and high-intensity operational demands.

Apollo is engineered to function as a "trusted collaborator" rather than a mere tool. Currently, the robot is designed to handle physically demanding and repetitive tasks in manufacturing and logistics, such as:

  • Transporting components and sorting
  • Kitting and down-stacking
  • Palletizing and trailer unloading

While the immediate focus remains on critical industrial workflows, Apptronik has outlined a long-term roadmap for Apollo to expand into the retail, healthcare, and domestic home environments.

Strategic Partnerships and Technological Pedigree

Apptronik’s technological edge is bolstered by a high-level strategic partnership with Google DeepMind. This collaboration aims to power the next generation of humanoid robots using Gemini Robotics, ensuring that Apptronik remains at the forefront of the embodied AI revolution. Furthermore, the company has secured partnerships with global brands such as Mercedes-Benz, GXO Logistics, and Jabil.

The company’s technical foundation is deeply rooted in advanced robotics research. Originating from the Human Centered Robotics Lab at the University of Texas at Austin, Apptronik’s expertise is built upon a decade of development and a portfolio of 15 previous robots, including work on NASA’s Valkyrie robot.

Looking Ahead

As Apptronik moves toward the debut of a highly anticipated new robot set to be unveiled in 2026, the company is aggressively investing in the infrastructure required for scale. This includes the development of state-of-the-art facilities dedicated to robot training and large-scale data collection.

With approximately 350 employees and a clear trajectory toward industrial automation at scale, Apptronik is positioned to redefine how human-robot interaction shapes the future of global labor and societal progress.
