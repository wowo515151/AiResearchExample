Investor Profile: LlamaIndex and the Evolution of LLM Data Frameworks

LlamaIndex, a private company operating within the Business/Productivity Software industry, is emerging as a significant player in the artificial intelligence sector. Headquartered in San Francisco, California, the company was founded in 2023 and currently maintains a team of 20 employees. LlamaIndex specializes in the development of a central interface toolkit designed to augment Large Language Model (LLM) applications with external data. Their platform serves as an agentic document workflow and data framework, providing advanced data connectors, secure cloud infrastructure, and customizable indexing. These tools empower developers to build scalable AI agents capable of performing document research, workflow automation, and complex insights generation.

The company has demonstrated consistent growth through several strategic funding rounds. LlamaIndex began its capital journey with a $1 million seed round in late 2022. This was followed by an $8.5 million seed round in June 2023, which was led by Greylock Partners. Notable angel investors participating in that round included industry figures such as Jack Altman, Lenny Rachitsky, Mathilde Collin (CEO of Front), Raquel Urtasun (CEO of Waabi), and Joey Gonzalez.

A significant milestone occurred on March 4, 2025, when LlamaIndex announced a $19 million Series A funding round. This round was led by Norwest Venture Partners, with continued participation from existing investor Greylock. At the time of this announcement, the company’s post-money valuation was approximately $93 million, bringing its total disclosed funding to $27.5 million. Since its inception, LlamaIndex has raised a total of approximately $28.5 million across its various funding stages.

Beyond traditional venture capital, LlamaIndex has secured strategic minority equity investments. In May 2025, the company received investment from Databricks (via Databricks Ventures' AI Fund) and KPMG LLP (via KPMG Ventures). Furthermore, in October 2025, LlamaIndex was selected as one of 40 companies for the AWS Generative AI Accelerator program. This non-equity engagement provided the company with access to the AWS AI technology stack, mentorship, and up to $1 million in AWS credits.

As the landscape for AI-driven enterprise intelligence intensifies, LlamaIndex faces competition from several notable firms, including LangChain, Aivo, RedBooth, Hugging Face, and Nuix. However, its specialized focus on providing the data framework necessary for LLM applications positions it as a critical infrastructure provider for developers looking to integrate complex enterprise documents into automated AI workflows.
