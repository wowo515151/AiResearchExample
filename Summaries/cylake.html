Investment Overview: Cylake’s Mission to Reclaim Cybersecurity Sovereignty

As global organizations face increasingly sophisticated, AI-led cyberattacks, a critical gap has emerged in the security architectures of the world’s largest and most regulated institutions. Many of these entities are unable to utilize public cloud-based security products due to stringent regulatory requirements and security concerns. This has forced them into a difficult choice: managing a fragmented patchwork of on-premises tools that lack visibility, or segmenting data to be sent to the public cloud, which creates exploitable vulnerabilities. Cylake is positioning itself to solve this dilemma by providing a complete, AI-native, data-driven cybersecurity platform designed specifically for these high-stakes environments.

The Solution: Sovereignty Through Integrated Architecture

Cylake’s platform is built on the principle of operational sovereignty. Unlike traditional security models that rely on public cloud infrastructure, Cylake’s solution is designed to operate entirely on-premises or within private cloud environments. This ensures that customers maintain full control over their data and operational workflows.

The company is developing an integrated data architecture that combines both hardware and software components to provide an end-to-end cybersecurity system. By utilizing a single data foundation, the platform provides deep analysis and "agentic protection" without the need for public cloud or public AI infrastructure. This approach allows organizations to maintain a full, transparent picture of their data and operations, ensuring optimal security and governance.

Leadership and Strategic Backing

The company is led by a team of experienced security engineers and executives, co-founded by industry veterans Nir Zuk, Wilson Xu, and Ehud (Udi) Shamir. This leadership team is backed by prominent investor Greylock Partners, signaling strong institutional confidence in Cylake’s vision to "bring cybersecurity back home" to the organizations that require the highest levels of data sovereignty.

Financial Position and Roadmap

Headquartered in Sunnyvale, United States, Cylake is currently in the Seed stage of development. To date, the company has raised a total of $45.0 million in funding, with its most recent funding round occurring in March 2026.

The platform is currently under development in consultation with several design partners. Cylake is working toward a significant milestone with the planned general availability of its platform in early 2027. For investors and industry observers, Cylake represents a specialized play on the intersection of AI-native security and the growing necessity for data sovereignty in the enterprise sector.
